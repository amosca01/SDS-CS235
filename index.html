Course Overview and Policies

Description

Visual analytics is the science of combining interactive visual interfaces and information visualization techniques with automatic algorithms to support analytical reasoning through human-computer interaction. People use visual analytics tools and techniques to synthesize information and derive insight from massive, dynamic, ambiguous, and often conflicting data. This course will serve as an introduction to the science and technology of visual analytics and will include lectures on both theoretical foundations and application methodologies. The goals of this course are for students to (1) develop a comprehensive understanding of this emerging, multidisciplinary field, and (2) apply that understanding toward a focused research problem in a real-world application or a domain of personal interest.

Learning Outcomes

By the end of this course students will be able to:
  • Critically evaluate inteactive data visualizations and suggest improvements and refinements
  • Apply a structured design process to create effective visualization tools
  • Conceptualize ideas and interaction techniques using sketching and prototyping
  • Use principles of human perception and cognition in visualization design
  • Develop interactive, data-driven visualizations using either programming in R, python, HTML5/JS, etc. (programming-intensive) or existing visual analytics software (non-programming track)
  • Work effectively as part of a cross-discplinary project team with members from SDS, CSC, and other learning traditions

Assessment

All assignments will be submitted through Gradescope: https://www.gradescope.com/courses/828757

Entry code: DK3X3R

There are a variety of assignments in this class: Homeworks, Mid-Semester Project, Final Project, and Participation and Engagement. The purpose of this is provide as many ways as possible for students to demonstrate mastery of material.

Homeworks

Homework is a chance to practice topics covered in lecture and receive feedback. See rubrics for grading guidelines. During the first half of the semester, the primary deliverables for this course will be weekly data challenges. A new dataset will be introduced during lecture, along with a set of guiding questions. Your task is to use what you know about data visualization and modeling to wrangle, explore, and ultimately report back your findings. Along the way, you'll document your analysis process, justify your visualization choices, and report any assumptions you make about the data.

Mid-Semester Project

The mid-semester project is a chance for students to work together and engage with an existing visual analytic tool. Student will work in groups to prepare a short (~5min) video case study of an existing visual analytics system: who built it, who it was designed for, how specific design choices shape the way the system works (or doesn't), and proposed changes or extensions for the tool.

Final Project

Final projects present the opportunity for students to apply the visualization techniques covered in class to a topic of interest to them. This assignment will be completed in groups. Grading will look at technical details, and creativity.

Participation and Engagement

Participation and engagement will take into account contributions in class which demonstrate intellectual curiosity or clear understanding of a topic, comments which help others in class to learn a difficult concept, timeliness and proactive communication about class attendance, and lab submissions.

Late Policy

All assignments are due as listed on the Course Schedule. There is a 24 hour gace period for late submissions, however you must be in class that day (or excused beforehand) and after the grace period late assignments will not be accepted.

If you have extenuating circumstances and need a more significant extension that is okay, however it is your responsibility to email Ab a minimum of 24hrs BEFORE the assignment is due to make arrangements. Not all requests will be granted, however there is a revise and resubmit policy (see below) for work students wish to improve.

Revise and Resubmit

There will be no regrades on assignments, or late submissions. However, if you are unhappy with a grade on an assignment you may revise and resubmit the assignment. The rules for resubmitting assignments are as follows:

  • You may not resubmitt and assignment on which you received a 0.
  • Your resubmission must note where changes were made and explain how the changes address the feedback you received on your first submission.
  • Your resubmission must include a thoughtful paragraph explaining what you learned from re-doing your assignment and how you will incorporate those lessons into future assignments.
  • You may only resubmit within 2 weeks of receiving a grade on an assignment.

Academic Integrity

Students are strongly encouraged to form study groups and to collaborate on assignments. The following information is required for all submitted work:

  • The names of all collaborating students be listed at the top of the submission. If you worked alone, please state: "I did not collaborate with anyone on this assignment."
  • A "References" section, with in-line citations to any resources you used. Citations should include page numbers (if a printed resource) or a direct URL (if an online resource). If you did not use any resources in completing the assignment, please state: "I did not utilize any external resources in completing this assignment."

You may consult online resources as part of your coursework, but you may not directly copy thoughts or code from online sources.

ChatGPT may not be used to perform the intellectual tasks required to complete an assignment. In other words, your writing and code must be your own, not generated by ChatGPT (this includes modifying code or text generated by ChatGPT - doing so is a violation of academic integrity for this course).

If you have a question about what is considered a violation of this policy, please ask!

You can find the colleges's academic integrity policy here: https://www.smith.edu/about-smith/offices/student-affairs/student-handbook/academic-honor-code-0.
